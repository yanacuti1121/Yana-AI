## 2026-06-08 | [confidence-level-labeling]

Research report delivered without confidence levels. Decision-maker treated all findings equally.

One finding: solid data from 3 independent sources, n=500 each. Another: single blog post from 2019, unclear methodology.

They got equal weight in the decision. The low-confidence finding drove a major product decision.

Every claim needs a confidence label: established (multiple sources, large n) vs speculative (single source, anecdotal) vs working hypothesis (plausible but untested).

**Muốn:**
- Skill `research-confidence-labeler` — auto-tag each claim with confidence level based on source quality, sample size, recency, independence
- Skill `source-triangulation-checker` — verify claims are supported by at least 3 independent sources before marking as established

Asked to research "AI coding tools market." Started reading everything. 4 hours in: 23 papers read, no synthesis, overwhelmed.

Scope first. Research question: "what are the primary adoption blockers for AI coding tools in enterprise teams?" Now each paper gets evaluated against that specific question.

Literature review without a specific research question is consumption, not research. The question is what makes it research.

**Muốnt:**
- Skill `research-question-sharpener` — help transform broad topic into specific, answerable research question before starting literature review
