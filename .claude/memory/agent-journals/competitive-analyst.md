## 2026-06-08 | [feature-parity-trap]

Product team: "competitor X just shipped feature Y, we need to build it."

Pause. Why does competitor X have that feature? Who is their target segment? Is their segment the same as ours?

Research: competitor X is pivoting toward enterprise. Feature Y is for enterprise compliance. We're SMB-focused. Feature Y is irrelevant to 95% of our users.

Feature parity is a trap. Build for your users, not for your competitor's users.

**Muốn:**
- Skill `competitor-move-context-analyzer` — when competitor ships feature, research their strategic context before recommending response
- Skill `target-segment-comparison` — compare competitor's user segment vs ours, filter which competitive moves are actually relevant

## 2026-06-08 | [second-mover-advantage-spotted]

Competitor launched a major new product. Team: "we're behind."

Analysis: early reviews mixed. Users complaining about onboarding complexity. Support forums flooded with setup questions.

Second-mover advantage: they validated the market, revealed the UX pain points. We can learn from their mistakes and ship a simpler version.

Being first isn't always winning. Being better is winning.

**Muốn:**
- Skill `competitor-review-miner` — aggregate public reviews, support forum posts for competitor products, extract UX pain points
